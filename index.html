BLUFF: A Benchmark for Linguistic Understanding of Fake-news Forensics

Jason Lucas, Dongwon Lee
Penn State University, College of Information Sciences and Technology
KDD 2026 — Datasets and Benchmarks Track
Code 🤗 Dataset

BLUFF spans 78 languages across 12 language families, covering both high-resource "big-head" (20) and low-resource "long-tail" (58) languages with 201K+ samples and 4 benchmark tasks.

Abstract

Disinformation spreads across linguistic boundaries, yet existing detection benchmarks are predominantly English-centric, leaving the global majority of languages unaddressed. We introduce BLUFF (Benchmark for Linguistic Understanding of Fake-news Forensics), a comprehensive multilingual benchmark spanning 78 languages across 12 language families, 10+ script types, and 4 syntactic typologies. BLUFF contains over 201,000 samples combining 122,836 human-written texts from IFCN-certified fact-checking organizations with 78,443 LLM-generated samples produced by 19 multilingual LLMs using our novel AXL-CoI (Adversarial Cross-Lingual Agentic Chain-of-Interactions) framework. The dataset features 4 content types (HWT, MGT, MTT, HAT), 39 textual modification techniques, and supports 4 benchmark tasks across veracity classification and authorship detection. We evaluate 5 multilingual encoders and 7 decoder models across 6 training settings, revealing significant performance gaps of 9–25% between high-resource and low-resource languages. Our quality filtering pipeline, mPURIFY, ensures dataset integrity through 32 features across 5 dimensions.
